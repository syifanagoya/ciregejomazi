FAQs About Andre Ducharme

Who is Andre Ducharme?

Andre Ducharme is a Canadian author, comedian, and humorist from Quebec. He was a member of the musical comedy group Rock et Belles Oreilles (RBO) from 1981 to 1995.

What are some of his notable works?

He has been involved in various roles throughout his career, including as a writer and actor for the series Radio Fictions en direct, co-host and humorist for Les Grandes Gueules and Le petit monde d’André Ducharme, and more.

He’s also known for his scriptwriting for the popular Quebec television special, Le Bye Bye.

Has he won any awards?

Yes, he has received numerous awards. He’s won Félix Awards for Best Television Show of the Year – Humor. The shows recognized were RBO 3.0 and Le Bye Bye 2007. Additionally, he’s received Gémeaux Awards.

These were for Best Humorous Special and Best Writing in Humor, also for Le Bye Bye 2007. Furthermore, he received the Médaille d’honneur from the National Assembly for RBO. There are other awards as well.

What is Un souper presque parfait, and what was his role in it?

Un souper presque parfait is a popular Quebec reality TV show based on the original English series Come Dine with Me.

He narrated the show for over 10 years and more than 1400 episodes, describing it as a significant part of his life and a project that allowed him to fully invest himself.

When did he leave Un souper presque parfait?

He recorded his last episode on November 3, after 10 and a half years with the show. He described his departure as the end of a beautiful adventure and expressed gratitude towards the show’s team, his partner Marie Hélène, and the audience.
