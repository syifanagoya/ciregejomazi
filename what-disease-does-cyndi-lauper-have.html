Who Is Cyndi Lauper?

Cyndi Lauper is a singer, songwriter, and actress, known for several all-time hit songs back in the 80s such as Time After Time and I Drove All Night.

Born on June 22, 1953, Lauper has sang on the world stage for over four decades, releasing many albums and hits back to back.

Some of her albums include She’s So Unusual (1983), which made top-five ranking on the Billboard Hot 100; “Girls Just Want to Have Fun”, “Time After Time”, “She Bop”, and “All Through the Night”.

Some of Lauper’s well known hit songs include “True Colors”, “Change of Heart”, and “I Drove All Night”.

Her hit song, “Time After Time” has ranked tops on VH1’s list of 100 Best Songs in the last 25 years.

So far, Lauper has sold more than 50 million records across the globe, and has won several global awards.

She is a recipient of the Tony Awards, the Grammy Awards, the Olivier Awards, the New York’s Outer Critics Circle, MTV Video Music Awards (VMAs), Billboard Awards, and American Music Awards (AMAs).

She is a Songwriters Hall of Famer and has been inducted into the Hollywood Walk of Fame,

Lauper is regarded as one of the Greatest Women of Rock & Roll, and is featured in the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame’s Women Who Rock exhibit.
